Water Lilies

Claude Monet

1896-1926

Water Lilies is a series of approximately 250 oil paintings by French Impressionist Claude Monet. The paintings depict Monet's flower garden at his home in Giverny, and were the main focus of Monet's artistic production during the last thirty years of his life.

Many of the works were painted while Monet suffered from cataracts. The series is among Monet's most famous works and his largest contribution to the art world. The paintings capture the natural beauty of his garden through different times of day, seasons, and weather conditions, showing Monet's fascination with the effects of natural light.

The Water Lilies demonstrate Monet's extraordinary ability to paint the same subject in different lighting conditions and times of day. They represent the culmination of Monet's interest in plein air landscape painting and his long-standing interest in the effects of light and atmosphere.
