Guernica

Pablo Picasso

1937

Guernica is a large 1937 oil painting on canvas by Spanish artist Pablo Picasso. It is one of his best-known works, regarded by many art critics as the most moving and powerful anti-war painting in history. It is exhibited in the Museo Reina Sofía in Madrid.

The painting depicts the bombing of Guernica, a Basque town in northern Spain, by Nazi German and Fascist Italian warplanes at the request of Spanish Nationalist forces during the Spanish Civil War. The scene shows people, animals, and buildings wrenched by violence and chaos.

Picasso's use of a monochromatic palette of black, white, and grey gives the painting a newspaper-like quality, emphasizing the immediacy and starkness of the tragedy. The fragmented and distorted figures reflect the Cubist style, while conveying the horror and chaos of war through symbolic imagery including a bull, a horse, and dismembered bodies.
