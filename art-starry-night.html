The Starry Night

Vincent van Gogh

1889

The Starry Night is an oil on canvas painting by Dutch Post-Impressionist painter Vincent van Gogh. Painted in June 1889, it depicts the view from the east-facing window of his asylum room at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, just before sunrise, with the addition of an idealized village.

The painting features a swirling night sky filled with flowing clouds, blazing stars, and a bright crescent moon. Below the rolling hills of the horizon lies a small town, while a large cypress tree dominates the left side of the canvas, reaching toward the sky like flames.

Despite being painted during van Gogh's stay at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum, The Starry Night is considered one of his finest works and is among the most recognized paintings in the history of Western culture. The work was painted during the day in van Gogh's ground-floor studio, as he was not allowed to paint in his bedroom.
